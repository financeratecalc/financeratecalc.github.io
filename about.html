Our Data Sources

Every calculation on this site is based on publicly available data from authoritative sources. Here's exactly where our numbers come from:

📊 Federal Reserve (FRED)
Mortgage rate history, economic indicators, interest rate data
🏠 NAR (National Association of Realtors)
Median home prices, metro area data, quarterly market reports
🏛️ HUD (Dept. of Housing & Urban Development)
FHA loan limits, affordability guidelines, lending standards
📈 Freddie Mac PMMS
Weekly mortgage rate survey — primary benchmark for rate data
🏢 US Census Bureau (ACS)
Household income, zip code demographics, homeownership rates
💼 Bureau of Labor Statistics
Wage data, CPI for inflation adjustments, employment statistics
🔵 Zillow Research
Current listing data, market trends, price history via Chrome Extension
🏦 CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau)
HMDA loan data, denial statistics, lender comparison data

Our Calculation Principles

🎯
Real Bank Math
We use the same DTI ratios, LTV thresholds, and underwriting criteria that actual lenders apply. Not simplified versions.
🔍
Transparent Assumptions
Every calculator shows its assumptions. National averages used where specific data isn't available. Always disclosed.
📅
Updated Regularly
Rate data updated weekly. Market data updated monthly. All content reviewed for accuracy against current lending standards.
⚠️
Educational Only
All tools are for educational purposes. Not financial advice. Always consult a licensed professional before making mortgage decisions.

Editorial & Review Process

All mortgage calculations are reviewed against current Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and FHA underwriting guidelines. Rate assumptions are updated weekly using Freddie Mac's Primary Mortgage Market Survey. City and affordability data is updated monthly using NAR and Census Bureau sources.

Content is written and reviewed by the founder — someone with direct underwriting experience — not outsourced to content writers. Every tool is tested with real mortgage scenarios before publication.
