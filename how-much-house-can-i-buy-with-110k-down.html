What $110,000 Actually Gets You

With $110,000 saved, you have three main paths. FHA gives you the most buying power ($3,143,000) but requires mortgage insurance for the life of the loan. Conventional at 5% down gets you $2,200,000 with PMI until you reach 20% equity. Putting the full 20% down limits you to $550,000 but eliminates PMI entirely — saving you $73,140 over the first 5 years.

⚠️ PMI Warning
At 5.0% down, you'll pay PMI of approximately $1,219/month until you reach 20% equity. That's $14,628/year that builds zero equity. Consider saving more before buying — or buying a smaller home at 20% down.

Credit Score Matters Too

Your down payment gets you in the door — your credit score determines the rate. A 620 score vs 740 on a $2,200,000 home means roughly $759,154 more in total interest over 30 years.
