Frequently Asked Questions

How much does rates need to drop before refinancing makes sense?
The old rule of thumb was "1% drop." The real answer depends on your break-even point: divide your closing costs by your monthly savings. If you'll stay in the home longer than that, refinance. A 0.75% drop with low closing costs can make sense; a 1.5% drop with high costs might not.
How do I track mortgage rates without checking every day?
Freddie Mac publishes the Primary Mortgage Market Survey every Thursday at freddiemac.com/pmms — the most reliable weekly benchmark. Set a calendar reminder to check it monthly. When rates approach your trigger number, start getting quotes.
Will refinancing hurt my credit score?
Each mortgage application is a hard inquiry — typically -5 to -10 points. However, multiple mortgage inquiries within a 14-day window count as just ONE inquiry (FICO's rate shopping protection). Shop aggressively within that window.
What's the current mortgage rate in 2026?
As of May 2026, 30-year fixed rates average approximately 6.4-6.8% depending on credit profile and lender. Check Freddie Mac's weekly survey for the most current benchmark. Your actual rate will vary based on your credit score, LTV ratio, and loan type.
