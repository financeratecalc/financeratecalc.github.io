What This Data Really Means

  • The US median household income is $74K — in 11 states, this isn't enough to buy the median home
  • With 10% down instead of 20%, the required salary jumps ~12% across the board
  • Remote work migration has pushed Idaho, Montana, and Nevada into six-figure territory
  • The gap between Hawaii (#1, $162K) and West Virginia (#50, $35K) is $127,000 in required salary
  • 28% is the bank's rule — your real comfortable number is closer to 25% to leave breathing room
  • Every 1% rate increase adds roughly $120-160/mo on a $300K loan — and $15-20K in required salary
