The Real Answer for Age 35

At 35, you're in the prime home-buying window. Income is usually growing, you have some savings, and a 30-year mortgage still pays off before retirement.

💡 Banker's Tip for Age 35
This is statistically the best age to buy. A 30-year mortgage matures right around retirement, building wealth at the perfect time.

What Banks Actually Look At

Banks cannot legally consider your age in lending decisions (Equal Credit Opportunity Act). What they DO look at: income stability, credit score, debt-to-income ratio, and down payment source. Your age only matters indirectly — through income level and credit history length.
