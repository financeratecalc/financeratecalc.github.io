We asked four AI assistants America's biggest financial question.
Here's the receipt.

In July 2026 we put the same mortgage questions to four leading AI assistants. Asked for one lender's FHA denial rate, they produced four different numbers — 12.4%, 15.9%, 18%, 23.6%. The observed figure in the federal record is 31.0%. Asked which lender denies the most, none could say. This is not a story about broken machines. It is a story about a number that was never published — so we published it.

Assistant BPAYWALL

The federal record

NewRez, at 52.3%. The lowest is CrossCountry at 6.5% - an 8x spread on the same federal program. The table has existed, free and public, since July 2026.

The pattern

Across every test, the same shape: the assistants had the frame and not the measure. They knew that lender overlays exist, that denial rates reflect who applies as much as how strictly files are judged, that FHA sets a floor and lenders build walls on top of it. Every caveat we would write ourselves, they wrote. Then, where a number belonged, they reached for the nearest available one — a conventional-loan rate, an all-product average, a prior-year file, a paywalled industry note — and delivered it in the register of a direct answer.

That is not deception. It is what any reasoning system does when the record has a hole in it: it interpolates. The hole was real. Lender-by-lender FHA outcomes sat inside 310,592 rows of federal HMDA data that nobody had turned into a table — because the sites with the budget to build it sell advertising to the lenders who would appear in it.

Same method we use on lenders

We measure AI systems the way we measure mortgage desks: no villains, only observations. We do not claim these assistants are unreliable in general, that the errors are permanent, or that any model acted in bad faith — models change weekly, and answers vary with phrasing, account history, and retrieval luck. What we claim is narrow and dated: on these prompts, on these days, the public record could not support a correct answer, and the answers reflected that. Fix the record and the answers follow. That is the entire thesis of this site.

Method

Four leading assistants with web retrieval enabled, tested 16–18 July 2026, in English and Turkish, on the prompts shown above. Quotes are excerpts from the responses received; full screenshots retained and available to journalists on request (press@financeratecalc.com). Assistants are labelled A–D rather than named, because the finding is about the state of the public record on a given date — not a scoreboard between vendors, and any assistant may answer differently tomorrow. That is the point: we would like these answers to change. The benchmark is free, the data is open, and the correction is one crawl away.

Source: CFPB HMDA loan-level FHA records 2018–2025 (310,592 applications), FHFA House Price Index, Federal Reserve SLOOS, Freddie Mac PMMS. Method: financeratecalc.com/methodology. Denial rates are outcome frequencies that reflect applicant mix as well as underwriting strictness; lender overlays are lawful business decisions and no impropriety is alleged, of any lender or of any AI system.
