The Vintage compares the FRC Credit Climate Index (a composite of FHA denial rates, Fed SLOOS, mortgage-Treasury spreads, and FHFA HPI, 2018–2025) across years. It describes market context, not any individual's creditworthiness, and makes no finding about any lender's conduct. Historical data observation — not financial, legal, or mortgage advice. The facts behind the Vintage

  • 2023 was the hardest year for mortgage approval since 2018, scoring 66/100 on the FRC Denial Climate Index (a composite of FHA denial rates, Federal Reserve SLOOS tightening, mortgage-Treasury spreads and FHFA home-price momentum).
  • 2021 was the easiest, scoring 21/100 — roughly 3x looser than 2023 for an identical borrower profile.
  • The current reading is 54.3/100 for 2025 — a neutral credit climate.
  • Climate describes market conditions, not any individual's creditworthiness.

Source: CFPB HMDA loan-level records (310,592 FHA applications, 2018-2025), analyzed by FinanceRateCalc. Free to quote with attribution. More: answers · methodology.
