Why black holes form

FHA guidelines set maximum DTI at 57% with compensating factors. No federal rule creates a 99% denial zone at DTI 36%. These are lender overlays — internal policies layered on top of federal rules. Each lender builds its own geometry. Some geometries have black holes. Some don't.

The critical insight: black holes are lender-specific, not borrower-specific. A profile that disappears at PennyMac may find 79% approval at CrossCountry. The loan doesn't change. The door does.
