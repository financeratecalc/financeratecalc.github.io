Buying a home has two walls.
Everyone measures the first. We measured the second.

The salary wall is famous: every year, a dozen sites publish "the salary you need to buy a home in every state." The second wall is invisible in those lists — whether the loan actually gets approved. This table shows both, side by side: the estimated salary needed for a median home, and the observed FHA denial rates from all 1,217,297 decisioned applications in the 2025 federal record.

The finding the salary lists hide: the two walls stack. In the 10 states requiring the highest salaries, 43.2% of small FHA applications (under $150K) were denied in 2025 — against 35.1% in the 10 cheapest states. Where houses cost the most, the cheapest route in is also the most likely to be refused: the small-loan penalty peaks at 3.19× in Idaho, and small-loan denial reaches 53.8% in New Hampshire. Two Americas, one federal program.
