FHA denial rates by state: all 52, ranked (2025)

Direct answer · updated 2026-07-19

Where you apply matters: FHA denial rates in 2025 ran from 29.0% in Hawaii to 14.8% in Idaho and Utah — roughly a 2× spread. But the sharper finding is inside each state: the small-loan penalty is worst where homes are most expensive. In Idaho, a sub-$150K FHA application was denied 3.2× as often as a $250K+ one. In New Hampshire, 53.8% of small FHA applications were denied — more than half. Source: the complete 2025 CFPB HMDA FHA dataset, 1,217,297 decisioned applications.

The two Americas of the starter loan

In affordable states (Iowa, Michigan, Illinois), a $120K loan buys an ordinary house, and the small-loan penalty is mild (~1.5×). In expensive states, a sub-$150K property is the market’s most marginal stock — aging, unusual, or failing FHA property standards at appraisal — and the penalty explodes: Idaho 3.2×, New Hampshire 2.7×, Utah 2.7×, Maine 2.7×, Oregon 2.6×, Massachusetts 2.6×. This is consistent with the national pattern we documented in the small-loan penalty, where collateral is a leading killer of small applications.

And in three markets — Hawaii, DC, and Alaska — fewer than 100 sub-$150K FHA applications received a decision all year. The affordable rung of the ladder hasn’t just gotten harder there. It has effectively vanished.

What this is not

State rates reflect the mix of lenders, property stock, and applicants in each market — not a ranking of “strict” or “lenient” states, and not a finding about any lender or regulator. The penalty pattern is an observation consistent with collateral and small-balance economics; we report what the federal record shows.
