📊 Why Bi-Weekly Works

There are 52 weeks in a year. Paying bi-weekly means 26 half-payments = 13 full monthly payments instead of 12. That one extra payment per year goes entirely toward principal — not interest.

The earlier in the loan you make extra principal payments, the more interest you avoid — because interest compounds daily on your remaining balance.

⚠️ Important: Some banks charge a fee to set up bi-weekly payments or require you to use their program. The alternative: just make one extra principal payment per year — same result, no fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I actually set up bi-weekly payments?
Call your lender's customer service and ask about "bi-weekly payment setup." Most major lenders (Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America) offer this either free or for a small fee. Alternatively, simply make one extra principal payment per year — same mathematical effect, no bank program required.
Is bi-weekly the same as paying half my monthly payment every 2 weeks?
Yes — exactly. The trick is that 52 weeks ÷ 2 = 26 payments, not 24. So you end up making 13 full payments per year instead of 12. That one extra payment goes entirely to principal.
Does my bank have to accept bi-weekly payments?
Not automatically. Some banks only process payments monthly and will hold your bi-weekly payment until the end of the month. Make sure to confirm with your lender that extra payments are applied directly to principal — and get it in writing.
What's better — bi-weekly payments or refinancing to a lower rate?
It depends on your current rate. If rates have dropped more than 1% below your current rate, refinancing usually wins. If rates are similar, bi-weekly payments are free and effective. Ideally, do both.
