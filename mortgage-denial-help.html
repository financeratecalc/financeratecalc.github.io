Why Did This Happen?

Federal guidelines (FHA, VA, Fannie Mae) set minimum standards. But every lender adds their own rules on top — called overlays. These aren't published anywhere. You only find out when you're denied.

Example: FHA minimum FICO is 580. But most lenders require 620, 640, or even 680 internally. They don't advertise this. You submit. You wait. You get denied. Now your credit score dropped from the hard pull.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I reapply after a denial?
Yes. A denial from one lender doesn't prevent you from applying elsewhere. In fact, applying to a lender whose historical profile better matches yours is often the solution.

How long should I wait after a denial?
There's no required waiting period for most denial reasons. However, if the denial was due to credit issues, giving your score time to recover before the next hard pull is worth considering.

Does a denial hurt my credit score?
The hard pull from the application may reduce your score slightly. If you apply to multiple lenders within a 45-day window, credit bureaus typically count them as one inquiry for mortgage purposes.

What is a lender overlay?
An overlay is a lender's internal rule that goes beyond federal minimums. FHA requires 580 FICO minimum — a lender might require 640. These overlays are not publicly disclosed and vary by lender.
