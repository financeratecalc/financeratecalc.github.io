--- 2) TWO WALLS PITCH (Scotsman/NMP/HousingWire; konu satiri dahil) ---
Subject: Data: the salary lists miss the second wall — denial risk stacks on price

Every year the "salary needed to buy a home in every state" lists make headlines.
Our analysis of the complete 2025 federal HMDA record (1.2M decisioned FHA applications)
shows a second wall the lists never mention: in the 10 states requiring the highest
salaries, 42.1% of small FHA applications (under $150K) were denied — vs 35.1% in the
10 cheapest states. The small-loan penalty peaks at 3.19x in Idaho; small-loan denial
hits 53.8% in New Hampshire. Where houses cost the most, the cheapest route in is the
most likely to be refused.
Full free table (CC BY 4.0): https://financeratecalc.com/salary-vs-denial-risk-by-state.html
Happy to provide state-level breakouts or custom cuts. — Ziya Yetis, FinanceRateCalc
(retired 23-yr banker; independent, no lender pays us)

--- 3) INCOMPLETE WALL PITCH (ayni listeye, ayri gun) ---
Subject: Two FHA lenders deny ~73% of files as "incomplete" — market median is 1.2%

In the 2025 federal record, "incomplete application" is a marginal FHA denial reason:
median share 1.2% across the 100 largest lenders. At two servicer-rooted lenders it is
the dominant one: Carrington 73.5%, Lakeview 72.2% (Planet Home 49.9%). Formal denials
only — administratively closed files are excluded, so this is not a paperwork artifact.
Story + methodology: https://financeratecalc.com/the-incomplete-wall.html

--- 7) METRO PITCH (en guclu mermi adayi — Cleveland acisi) ---
Subject: Data: in Cleveland, FHA denial odds swing 74 points depending on the lender

New from the complete 2025 federal HMDA record: the first free, FHA-specific metro denial
table (320 MSAs). Headline finding: the famous lender spread lives INSIDE cities. In
Cleveland, the five highest-volume FHA lenders ranged from 6.3% (CrossCountry) to 80.1%
(AmeriSave) denial in 2025 - 73.8 points inside one metro. Pittsburgh: 72.7 points.
Metro denial rates themselves ran 8.9% (Amarillo, TX) to 31.8% (Bridgeport-Stamford, CT),
and the small-loan penalty peaks at 3.61x in El Paso (32.1% vs 8.9%).
Existing metro studies track approvals or demographics; none publishes the FHA loan-size
or lender axis. Full table + open JSON (CC BY 4.0):
