What Salary Do You Need to Own in South Beach?

Using the standard 28% front-end housing ratio, you need $264,745/year in gross income to comfortably afford the median home in South Beach at current rates. That's the salary required just for housing — before food, transportation, retirement savings, or healthcare.

The median household income in Miami is approximately $145,610/year. That means the typical Miami household is $119,135 short of what's needed to buy in South Beach.

What Most Buyers Miss About South Beach

The listing price is only the beginning. Property taxes in Miami run approximately 1.1% of assessed value — on a $780,000 home, that's $715/month before you've paid a dollar toward the mortgage.

Homeowners insurance in this market averages $700/month — higher than national averages due to local risk factors. HOA fees in South Beach typically run $900/month for common area maintenance and amenities.

Add a 1% annual maintenance reserve (the industry standard) and your true all-in monthly cost reaches $6,827 — not the $3,862 the mortgage payment calculator shows.
