The Real Answer for Age 27

At 27, you have time on your side but likely limited savings. Focus on building a 10-20% down payment and keeping student loan payments low relative to income.

💡 Banker's Tip for Age 27
FHA loans (3.5% down) are your best friend at this age. Your biggest risk is taking on too much too soon.

What Banks Actually Look At

Banks cannot legally consider your age in lending decisions (Equal Credit Opportunity Act). What they DO look at: income stability, credit score, debt-to-income ratio, and down payment source. Your age only matters indirectly — through income level and credit history length.
