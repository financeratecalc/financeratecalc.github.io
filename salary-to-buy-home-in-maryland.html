The Real Numbers in Maryland

The median home price in Maryland is $398,000. With a 20% down payment and a 6.9% mortgage rate, your monthly PITI payment comes to $2,609/month — including principal, interest, property taxes (1.09% avg.), and insurance.

To qualify under standard bank underwriting (28% front-end DTI), you need a gross annual income of at least $112,000. Maryland's median household income is approximately $90,000/year, giving the state a price-to-income ratio of 4.4×.

At 4.4×, Maryland is one of the more affordable states in the country — most buyers earning the median income can qualify for the median home.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do I need to buy a house in Maryland in 2026?
To afford the median Maryland home ($398,000) with a 20% down payment and 28% front-end DTI ratio, you need at least $112,000/year in gross income. If you have existing debt (car payments, student loans), you may need more.
What is the average home price in Maryland?
The median home price in Maryland is approximately $398,000 as of 2026, based on NAR and Zillow Research data. Prices vary significantly by city and region within the state.
Is Maryland a good state to buy a home in 2026?
Maryland currently shows WARM buyer demand based on FRC listing analysis. With WARM demand, the market is balanced. Well-priced homes move but buyers still have room to negotiate. The price-to-income ratio of 4.4× suggests strong affordability relative to the national average.
What is the down payment for a house in Maryland?
For the median Maryland home: 20% conventional down payment = $79,600. FHA minimum 3.5% = $13,930. FHA requires a minimum 580 credit score. Conventional loans typically require 620+.
