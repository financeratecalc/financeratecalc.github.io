🏛️
Co-op DTI is cultural, not scientific
Co-op boards set DTI limits through bylaws — not actuarial models. The 25% figure has no federal backing. It reflects building culture, not empirical default risk data.
📊
FHA 45% DTI has decades of data
FHA's DTI limits are derived from loan performance data across millions of borrowers. The 45% threshold reflects observed default risk — not a board's cultural preference.
🚪
Same person, two outcomes
A nurse earning $95k/year with 40% DTI qualifies for FHA financing nationwide — but is screened out of most Manhattan co-op inventory. Not due to risk. Due to geography.
🔍
Neither world sees the other
Co-op brokers rarely work with FHA buyers. FHA lenders rarely deal with co-op inventory. These markets evolved separately — creating a permanent information gap that costs borrowers options.
The core observation

DTI is not an objective risk measure.
It is a cultural artifact.

The same 40% DTI that makes you "too risky" for a Park Avenue co-op makes you a "solid applicant" at Guild Mortgage. The borrower didn't change. The institution's framework did. This is the DTI culture clash.
