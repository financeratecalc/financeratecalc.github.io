Who Can Afford to Live
Near Their Employer?

By Ziya Y. · 23 Years Banking · BLS OEWS + Zillow ZHVI · May 2026

The FRC Corporate Affordability Index (CAI) measures what percentage of local housing is affordable on the median employee salary. We analyze 12 major US employers — from LAUSD teachers to Google engineers.

How CAI works: HIGHER score = MORE affordable.
CAI 72 means 72% of local homes are affordable on the median salary. CAI 6 means only 6% are affordable — a housing crisis.
Formula: CAI = homes where PITI ≤ 36% of median monthly salary / total local inventory × 100
Assumptions: 6.7% mortgage rate, 20% down payment, local property tax + insurance rates.

📐 Methodology

Key Findings

Teachers and nurses face the worst crisis. SFUSD teachers (CAI 6) and LAUSD teachers (CAI 8) can afford fewer than 10% of homes near their schools.

Even $175K tech salaries aren't enough. Google engineers in Mountain View can only afford 18% of local homes. Apple (16%) and Meta (14%) engineers face similar barriers.

The one bright spot: Mayo Clinic, Rochester MN. Healthcare workers earning $92K can afford 72% of local homes — what a functional housing market looks like.
