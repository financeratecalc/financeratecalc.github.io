🏠 The Housing-Employment Disconnect

In 2026, the average American worker commutes 26 minutes each way. But in major metro areas, the gap between where people work and where they can afford to live has become one of the defining crises of our time. A teacher in San Francisco earns $84,000 — and can afford housing in exactly 3% of the city they serve. A nurse in New York earns $95,000 — and qualifies for a mortgage in 7% of the metro area where they work. This map tracks the disconnect, employer by employer.
