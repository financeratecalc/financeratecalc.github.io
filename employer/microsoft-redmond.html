The Reality

Even at $158,000 — nearly 3x the US median — a Microsoft employee faces a housing affordability crisis in the Seattle metro. The median Redmond home requires 35% of gross income, right at the upper edge of safe borrowing.

Redmond's housing market has been transformed by Microsoft's growth and the influx of Amazon, Google, and other tech employers to the Puget Sound region. The cumulative effect has pushed housing costs to levels that strain even high-earning tech workers — particularly those with student debt, single-income households, or earlier career stages.
