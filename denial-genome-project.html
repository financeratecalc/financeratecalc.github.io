What is a decision gene?

A decision gene is a measurable behavioral trait extracted from a lender's historical approval and denial patterns. Just like a human gene codes for a specific trait — eye color, height — a lender's decision gene codes for a specific underwriting behavior.

We extract these genes from CFPB HMDA data: cliff points, variance slopes, stability scores, credit sensitivity, CLTV thresholds, income tolerance. Each one is a gene. Together they form a lender's decision DNA.

The gene map — what we track

GENE · CLIFF-85
CLTV Cliff
A hard behavioral threshold at 85% loan-to-value. Rocket's denial rate jumps +54.9pp above this threshold (2025). CrossCountry: +47.5pp. MrCooper: +1.7pp — effectively absent. Source: 310,592 HMDA FHA records, 2023–2025.
↑ +54.9pp denial jump at 85% LTV (Rocket 2025) · MrCooper: +1.7pp
GENE · VAR-HI
High Variance
Decision inconsistency across similar borrower profiles. A high-variance lender may approve 580-score borrowers in Q1 and deny them in Q3 with no guideline change.
↑ Unpredictable approval odds
GENE · STAB-LO
Low Stability
Behavioral drift over 12-month windows. Lenders with this gene change their overlay thresholds frequently — what worked 6 months ago may not work today.
↑ High routing risk in volatile markets
GENE · DTI-SENS
DTI Sensitivity
Amplified denial response to debt-to-income ratios above 43% — beyond what FHA/Fannie minimum requirements specify. The lender applies its own overlay.
↑ 2.3× denial rate at DTI 43–50%
GENE · FICO-CLIFF
Credit Score Cliff
A hard floor above the agency minimum. Lender may nominally accept 580 FICO (FHA floor) but behavioral data shows near-zero approvals below 620.
↑ Hidden floor 40 points above stated minimum
GENE · GEO-BIAS
Geographic Concentration
Behavioral preference or aversion toward specific metro markets. Some lenders quietly pull back from high-denial-rate metros without announcing a policy change.
↑ 22% lower approval in flagged metros

Why nobody did this before

HMDA data has been public since 1975. But nobody treated lender behavior as a genetic system — extracting features, classifying traits, building comparable profiles. The academic literature focuses on fair lending disparities. The industry focuses on rate sheets. Nobody mapped the behavioral DNA of the decision itself.

We did. 2.9 million records. 8 years. 100+ features per lender. This is the first structured behavioral genome for the US mortgage market.
