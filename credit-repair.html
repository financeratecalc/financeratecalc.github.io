FAQ

Common Questions — Honest Answers

Is credit repair legal?
Yes — completely. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives you the legal right to dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information on your credit report. Credit repair companies like Sky Blue do this on your behalf. What's NOT legal is creating a new credit identity (CPN fraud) or paying to have accurate negative information removed.
Can accurate negative items be removed?
Generally no — if an item is accurate, verifiable, and within the reporting window (7 years for most negatives, 10 for bankruptcies), it stays. What CAN be removed: errors in dates, amounts, or account status; items the creditor can't verify; duplicates; and accounts that aren't yours. This is what legitimate credit repair focuses on.
How long does credit repair take?
Bureaus must respond to disputes within 30–45 days by law. Most clients see initial results within 35–60 days. Full improvement — especially for multiple negative items — typically takes 3–6 months. The timeline depends on how many items are disputed and how quickly creditors respond.
Can I do this myself instead of paying Sky Blue?
Yes — you have the same legal rights to dispute items directly with bureaus. It's free. The reason many people use a service is time, knowledge, and persistence. Sky Blue knows what language to use, which items to dispute first, and how to follow up effectively. If you're comfortable researching the process and writing detailed dispute letters, DIY is absolutely a valid option.
Will credit repair hurt my score?
No — disputing items does not lower your score. If items are removed, your score improves. If disputes are verified and items stay, your score is unchanged. The process itself has no negative impact.
How much can my score improve?
It depends entirely on what's on your report. Removing a single collection could add 20–50 points. Removing multiple negative items could add 80–120 points. There's no guaranteed number — anyone who promises a specific score increase is not being honest with you.
