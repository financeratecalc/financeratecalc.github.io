Shadow Approvals: the denials that shouldn't have happened.

Data: CFPB HMDA 2018–2025 · 3,075,654 FHA applications · 11 major lenders
Method: cell-level counterfactual excess-denial analysis, risk-adjusted top-quartile benchmark
First published definition of the term "Shadow Approval" — FRC Intelligence

A Shadow Approval is a mortgage denial that occurred in a lending environment where materially lower denial outcomes were simultaneously observed elsewhere in the same market. The borrower was told no — while a statistical yes existed in the same system, at the same time. We counted them.

2025: 11,835 Shadow Approvals — 32 Americans per day denied while a statistical approval existed in the same market.

The strangest year: 2021

In 2021 — the cheapest money in modern history, the loosest market our index has ever measured (OFI 8) — nearly half of all FHA denials were Shadow Approvals. Easy markets don't fix strict lenders. Institutional identity overrides market conditions, which is consistent with our 9+2 structural finding that lender identity explains ~80% of denial variance.

The hopeful trend: the Shadow Approval rate has fallen from 48.8% (2021) to 11.8% (2025). The market is self-correcting — Wells Fargo's FHA volume collapsed, PennyMac structurally improved. But 32 people a day is still 32 people a day.

Methodology — built against ourselves

We deliberately made the count conservative. The benchmark is not the best lender, but the 25th-percentile lender rate, multiplied by a 1.5× risk premium — explicitly conceding that the denied pool is marginally riskier than average applicants. The count is cell-level per lender (no cross-lender netting: another applicant's approval does not undo your denial). Lender-years under 1,000 applications are excluded. Every input is public CFPB HMDA data, and the full computation is archived and reproducible.

What this is not: a claim that any individual borrower would have been approved elsewhere — HMDA does not track the same file across lenders. Shadow Approvals describe a statistical population, not individual counterfactuals. HMDA does not track individuals across lenders. Shadow Approvals are a statistical population — the excess denials a market produces above its own disciplined baseline.
